# UX Rules — Batch 4 (Parts 18-22)

## RULE: Value-impact
**Chapter:** 52. Video (inferred from context)
**✅ Correct:** The video player shows a "Skip Ads" button giving users control over ad viewing experience.
**❌ Wrong:** The video plays with an ad and no visible option for the user to skip or dismiss it.
**Key fix:** Provide a "Skip Ads" button so users can choose to engage with or bypass ads.
---

## RULE: Use captions and transcripts
**Chapter:** 52. Video
**✅ Correct:** The video page includes a visible "Transcript" section below the video for accessibility.
**❌ Wrong:** The video page shows no captions or transcript option, leaving non-hearing users without access.
**Key fix:** Always provide captions and/or a transcript alongside video content.
---

## RULE: Put users in control and prevent autoplay
**Chapter:** 52. Video
**✅ Correct:** A feed shows a grid of video thumbnails that require a tap to play, giving users control.
**❌ Wrong:** Videos in a feed begin autoplaying immediately without user initiation.
**Key fix:** Do not autoplay videos; require an explicit user action to start playback.
---

## RULE: Avoid rapidly flashing videos and animations
**Chapter:** 52. Video
**✅ Correct:** A photosensitive warning screen appears before a flashing video, offering "Watch Video" or "Skip all" options.
**❌ Wrong:** A video with rapidly flashing, strobing light animations plays without any warning.
**Key fix:** Display a photosensitive warning before any video with flashing or strobing content.
---

## RULE: Indicate elements by action
**Chapter:** 53. Menus
**✅ Correct:** Hovering over the hamburger icon shows a tooltip "Show/hide navigation menu" describing its function.
**❌ Wrong:** Hovering over the hamburger icon shows a tooltip "Hamburger Menu" which just names the element.
**Key fix:** Label interactive elements by what they do, not what they are called.
---

## RULE: Use cascading menu for faster navigation
**Chapter:** 53. Menus
**✅ Correct:** A multi-level nav menu uses fly-out submenus so users can navigate directly to subcategories.
**❌ Wrong:** A flat menu forces users to click through multiple pages to reach deeply nested items.
**Key fix:** Use cascading submenus for deep navigation structures to reduce the number of clicks needed.
---

## RULE: Use simple navigation
**Chapter:** 53. Menus
**✅ Correct:** A vertical menu shows all navigation options in a clean list without overflow controls.
**❌ Wrong:** A vertical menu overflows its container, hiding items behind a scrollable or clipped region.
**Key fix:** Keep navigation menus simple and fully visible without requiring scrolling to see all items.
---

## RULE: Place icons before labels
**Chapter:** 53. Menus
**✅ Correct:** Menu items show the icon to the left of the label (e.g., undo icon then "Undo" text).
**❌ Wrong:** Menu items show the label first and then the icon to its right, reversing the natural reading order.
**Key fix:** Always place the icon before the label in menu items for consistent visual scanning.
